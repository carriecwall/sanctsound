Smaller Vessels in CINMS

Sights & Sounds

A variety of vessels are common in the waters around the Channel Islands, such as the R/V Shark Cat which supports science and research activities. Photo: NOAA

These engine sounds from a small boat were recorded in CINMS. Noise from this vessel impacts frequencies below 6,000 Hz the most but is present up to 15,000 Hz.

Time series

The sounds of vessels were detected at listening station CI05 in CINMS in all but 3 weeks during the study period. Vessel sounds include those produced by larger commercial ships as well as smaller recreational boats. More vessels were detected in the summer months and least often in the winter months.

Daily Patterns

Vessel sounds were detected all hours of the day at listening station CI05 in CINMS during the study period. Vessels were heard more frequently in the afternoon and evening and less frequently in the morning.

Monthly Patterns

Vessel sounds were heard at listening station CI05 in CINMS every month of the year during the study period. Vessels were heard most often in April, May and June and least often in March and August.
